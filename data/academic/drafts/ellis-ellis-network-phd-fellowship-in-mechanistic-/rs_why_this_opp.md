## Why ELLIS Network

The ELLIS Network's emphasis on mechanistic interpretability directly resonates with my background in applied machine learning, particularly in the context of large language models for decision-making. This PhD opportunity offers a unique chance to explore how behaviours and decision rules can be made interpretable, aligning with both my MSc dissertation on supply chain information sharing and my extensive work in regulated environments.

Utilising frameworks such as DeBERTa-v3, as demonstrated in my dissertation, I have experienced the critical need for transparency in LLM outputs. My project focused on enhancing understanding through sentiment analysis and leveraging Ethereum smart contracts, directly applicable to ensuring model trustworthiness and security. 

The University of Copenhagen's research environment is well-suited to my aspirations, given its commitment to advancing responsible AI. I am eager to contribute to ground-breaking insights in LLM security, especially through robust interpretability methods.
