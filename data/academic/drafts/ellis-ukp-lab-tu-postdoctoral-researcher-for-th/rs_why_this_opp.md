## Why UKP Lab, TU

Joining the UKP Lab at TU Darmstadt to work on the ERC Advanced Grant InterText aligns seamlessly with my research trajectory, especially in the realm of natural language processing (NLP) and large language models (LLMs). The integration of LLMs into understanding textual data, as proposed in the project, resonates with my experience developing transformer-based models, particularly through my MSc dissertation under Dr. Ammar Al-Bazi, where I harnessed DeBERTa-v3 for analysis within supply chain frameworks. 

Moreover, my expertise in sentiment analysis and smart contract implementation provides a strong foundation for exploring the intricacies of textual data interpretation and user behaviour modelling, essential components highlighted in the project description. The UKP Lab's focus on pioneering research in applied algorithms and its commitment to pushing the boundaries of LLM applications demonstrates a compelling environment where I can contribute effectively. My existing projects, such as the LLM-grounded decision-rule generator, illustrate my ability to innovate at the intersection of machine learning and practical applications, making me a fitting candidate for this role.
