## Why Newcastle University

Newcastle University's School of Engineering stands at the forefront of urban transport innovation, particularly in modelling the interplay between transport behaviour and urban infrastructure. This aligns seamlessly with my MSc dissertation under Dr Ammar Al-Bazi at Aston University, where I explored blockchain technology integrated with large language models to improve supply chain management decisions. My targeted focus on behavioural responses within transport systems directly complements the research mandate of this position, which seeks to enhance urban knowledge through innovative modelling frameworks.

My GitHub projects, particularly the Crowd-Shipping-ABM and Mini-Digital-Twin repos, demonstrate my familiarity with agent-based modelling (ABM) and simulation methodologies that can be pivotal for developing effective urban knowledge models. These prototypes reflect my understanding of integrating human factors into transport systems, a gap that the role at Newcastle explicitly aims to address.

Engaging in research that prioritises human behaviour in urban logistics aligns closely with my professional aspirations and lived experiences growing up in Bandung, where transport inefficiencies shaped my keen interest in sustainable, integrated transport solutions. I am eager to contribute to Newcastle University's efforts in developing forward-thinking, robust urban transport methodologies.
