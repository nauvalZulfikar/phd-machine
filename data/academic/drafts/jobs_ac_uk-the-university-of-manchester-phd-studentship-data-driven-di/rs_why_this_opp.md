## Why The University of Manchester

The University of Manchester's emphasis on Data-Driven Digital Twins for Measured Energy Systems aligns with my research trajectory, specifically integrating agent-based modelling and optimisation to drive behaviourally-informed insights in transport systems. My prior experience in using Python and simulation frameworks for urban logistics modelling directly correlates to the methodologies outlined in this studentship.

Utilising my expertise in data analytics gained from my MSc in Business Analytics, I aim to apply large language model (LLM) techniques alongside advanced simulation to enhance the scalability and responsiveness of digital twin frameworks. Moreover, my work with crowd-shipping and integrated transport solutions provides a solid foundation in addressing energy consumption metrics critical for the project’s objectives.

Collaborating to refine these data-driven methodologies at Manchester, known for its cutting-edge research in engineering and technology, will not only fulfill my ambition to innovate in the field but also contribute to the practical applications sought in modern energy systems and urban transport strategies.
