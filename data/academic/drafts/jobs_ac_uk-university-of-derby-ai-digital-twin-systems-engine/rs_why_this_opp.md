## Why University of Derby

The University of Derby's commitment to innovation in AI and Digital Twin frameworks directly aligns with my research focus on integrated passenger-freight transport systems. My prior research, particularly through my MSc dissertation under Dr Ammar Al-Bazi at Aston University, highlighted the critical importance of behavioural factors in transport systems, specifically aiming to develop decision rules through LLM analysis. This aligns with the University of Derby's approach that utilises modern AI methodologies to enhance understanding and operational efficiency in transport.

My experience in agent-based modelling, as demonstrated in my GitHub repo 'Crowd-Shipping-ABM', positions me uniquely for this role. This project specifically models behavioural interactions in logistics, resonating with the potential applications of digital twins in urban transport as described in the job posting. Furthermore, my knowledge of simulation techniques provides a solid foundation to contribute effectively to the development of digital twin systems that respond to real-world challenges in urban logistics.

Engaging with the University of Derby’s research initiatives would allow me to further contribute to advancing knowledge in urban transport systems, particularly through the integration of AI-driven models that cater to the pressing needs of sustainable transport.
