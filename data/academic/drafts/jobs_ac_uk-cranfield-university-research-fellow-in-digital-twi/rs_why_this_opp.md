## Why Cranfield University

Cranfield University's emphasis on decarbonising transport through innovative digital twinning frameworks aligns seamlessly with my research experience and professional background. During my MSc at Aston University, I developed systems for improving supply chain information sharing, particularly focusing on blockchain integration and large language models (LLMs) under the supervision of Dr Ammar Al-Bazi. This foundational work parallels the need for effective computational models in understanding transport behaviours and optimising logistics systems as highlighted in your posting.

The application of digital twins in the transport sector requires advanced modelling techniques that I have explored comprehensively through agent-based and discrete-event simulations. My experienced use of tools such as mesa for agent-based modelling and simpy for discrete-event simulations equips me well for this project. Furthermore, my ongoing work on crowd-shipping models can directly enhance the frameworks you aim to advance at Cranfield, ensuring both technical feasibility and behavioural insights in urban transport systems.

Working alongside Cranfield’s distinguished faculty would not only allow me to contribute significantly to decarbonisation efforts but also enable me to refine my skills in an environment dedicated to cutting-edge research.
