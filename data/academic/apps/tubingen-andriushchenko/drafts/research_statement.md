## Abstract

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) systems has brought about significant benefits across various domains. However, the increasing complexity and autonomy of these systems have raised concerns regarding their safety and robustness, particularly in adversarial settings. This research aims to address these concerns by developing a framework for trustworthy AI systems that can withstand adversarial attacks and maintain alignment with human values. Building on the foundational work of Dr. Maksym Andriushchenko in AI safety and adversarial robustness, this study will explore the integration of adversarial training techniques, robust optimisation, and safety evaluations to enhance the resilience of AI models. The research will also investigate the use of large language models (LLMs) for generating adaptive decision rules that improve system robustness in dynamic environments. By focusing on both technical and behavioural aspects, this project seeks to contribute to the development of AI systems that are not only technically sound but also socially responsible and aligned with human values.

---

## 1. Introduction and Problem Statement

The deployment of AI systems in critical applications necessitates a focus on their safety and robustness, especially in adversarial environments where malicious actors may attempt to exploit system vulnerabilities. Dr. Maksym Andriushchenko's recent work highlights the importance of adversarial robustness and trustworthy machine learning (Andriushchenko et al., 2023; Andriushchenko et al., 2024; Andriushchenko et al., 2025). Despite advancements in adversarial training and robust optimisation, challenges remain in ensuring that AI systems can effectively resist adversarial attacks while maintaining alignment with human values. This research addresses these challenges by proposing a comprehensive framework that integrates adversarial robustness techniques with safety evaluations and behavioural modelling.

---

## 2. Research Questions

Three interconnected research questions guide this study:

**RQ1, Adversarial Robustness.** How can adversarial training techniques be optimised to enhance the robustness of AI systems against a wide range of adversarial attacks?

**RQ2, Safety Evaluations.** What methodologies can be employed to systematically evaluate the safety and alignment of AI systems with human values in dynamic and adversarial environments?

**RQ3, Behavioural Modelling.** How can large language models be utilised to generate adaptive decision rules that improve the robustness and safety of AI systems in real-world scenarios?

---

## 3. Literature Review

**Adversarial Robustness.** The field of adversarial robustness has seen significant progress, with techniques such as adversarial training and robust optimisation being widely studied (Goodfellow et al., 2015; Madry et al., 2018). Dr. Andriushchenko's work has contributed to this area by exploring novel adversarial training methods that enhance model resilience (Andriushchenko et al., 2023; Andriushchenko et al., 2024). However, challenges remain in developing models that can withstand diverse and evolving adversarial strategies.

**Safety Evaluations.** Ensuring the safety and alignment of AI systems with human values is a critical concern. Recent studies have proposed various safety evaluation frameworks, but there is a need for more comprehensive approaches that consider both technical and behavioural aspects (Amodei et al., 2016; Leike et al., 2018). Dr. Andriushchenko's research in trustworthy machine learning provides a foundation for developing such frameworks (Andriushchenko et al., 2025).

**Behavioural Modelling.** The use of large language models for generating adaptive decision rules is an emerging area of research. Studies have shown the potential of LLMs in improving system robustness by adapting to dynamic environments (Brown et al., 2020; Radford et al., 2021). This research will build on these findings to explore the integration of LLMs with adversarial robustness techniques.

## 8. Challenges and Limitations

1. **Complexity of Adversarial Attacks:** The evolving nature of adversarial attacks may pose challenges in developing robust models. Mitigation: Regularly update training datasets and algorithms to address new attack vectors.

2. **Ethical Considerations:** Balancing technical advancements with ethical implications can be challenging. Mitigation: Engage with diverse stakeholders to ensure ethical considerations are integrated into the research.
